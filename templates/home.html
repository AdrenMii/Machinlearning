FOUNDATION

Technological Foundation

Machine Learning is a branch of Artificial Intelligence that enables systems to learn patterns from data. In video games, this means that characters, environments and difficulty systems can evolve dynamically based on player behavior.

Unlike traditional rule-based systems, machine learning introduces adaptability, optimization and probabilistic decision-making.

OVERVIEW

Types of Machine Learning Applied

Type How it works Application in games Impact
Supervised Learns from labeled data. Player behavior prediction. Personalized experience.
Unsupervised Identifies patterns without labels. Playstyle segmentation. Design optimization.
Reinforcement Learns through rewards and penalties. NPCs that improve strategy. Greater realism and unpredictability.
EVOLUTION

Traditional Problems vs ML Solutions

Before Machine Learning, video games relied on manually programmed behaviors, resulting in repetitive patterns and little variability.

1
Predictable NPCs

Before

Fixed scripts and decision trees made NPCs completely predictable and easy to exploit.

After ML

Reinforcement learning allows NPCs to adapt strategies, learn from the player and become genuinely unpredictable.

2
Limited Scenarios

Before

Every map and level had to be hand-crafted, limiting the amount of content a team could produce.

After ML

Procedural generation algorithms create infinite unique worlds, levels and missions automatically.

3
Unbalanced Difficulty

Before

Fixed easy/medium/hard settings failed to match each individual player's actual skill level.

After ML

Adaptive systems monitor performance in real time and adjust difficulty dynamically to keep the player engaged.
