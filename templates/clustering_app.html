Preprocessing & Model Configuration

Data Preprocessing

StandardScaler was applied to all three features before training. This transforms each feature to have mean = 0 and standard deviation = 1, preventing high-magnitude features (e.g., annual income) from dominating the distance calculations.

INTERPRETATION

Results Interpretation

Cluster 1 — Young Shoppers
High Engagement, Low Income

Young customers (~25 yrs) with lower incomes but very high spending scores. They are frequent, enthusiastic shoppers who respond well to promotions, discounts, and loyalty programs. Best targets for flash sales and social-media campaigns.

Cluster 2 — Mid-Career
Balanced Profile

Mid-career professionals (~40 yrs) with moderate income and moderate spending. They are selective buyers who value quality and convenience. Best targeted with premium mid-range products, bundles, and subscription offers.

Cluster 3 — Senior Spenders
High Income, Conservative

Senior customers (~55 yrs) with high incomes but low spending scores. They are deliberate, quality-driven buyers with strong brand loyalty. Best engaged with premium products, exclusive memberships, and personal service.

Business Value

K-Means clustering transformed a flat 1 200-record dataset into three actionable customer personas without any prior labeling. Each persona can now receive differentiated marketing messages, product recommendations, and pricing strategies — directly increasing conversion rates and customer lifetime value. This demonstrates the core business power of unsupervised learning.
