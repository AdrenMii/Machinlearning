Procedural generation allows creating maps, levels, missions and entire worlds automatically using algorithms, mathematical noise and ML models, without needing to design them manually one by one.

FOUNDATION

What is procedural generation?

It is a technique where game content — terrain, dungeons, objects, missions or even music — is generated in real time using mathematical rules and algorithms instead of being hand-crafted by artists.

Combined with Machine Learning, models can learn what type of content is most engaging for each player and dynamically generate personalized experiences.

TECHNIQUES

Main techniques used

Perlin Noise

Generates natural terrain and height maps with smooth, realistic variations. Used in Minecraft.

BSP Trees

Recursively divides spaces to generate dungeons, rooms and corridors coherently.

L-System Grammars

Simulates plant growth, trees and organic structures using rewriting rules.

Generative Networks (GAN)

Deep learning models that generate entirely new textures, maps and scenarios by learning from existing data.

ANALYSIS

Advantages and disadvantages

✅ Advantages

Infinite content

Generates more content than a human team could ever create manually.

Reduces costs

Fewer artists and designers needed to build the world.

High replayability

Every playthrough is different, keeping the game fresh for longer.

Personalization

With ML, adapts content to each player's style.

❌ Disadvantages

Lack of narrative

Generated content may lack story and clear purpose.

Inconsistent quality

Can generate boring or meaningless areas if not well calibrated.

Hard to control

It's complex to guarantee the result is always playable and balanced.

Requires tuning

Parameters must be carefully adjusted to avoid repetitive results.
