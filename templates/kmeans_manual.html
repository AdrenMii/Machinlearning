The dataset contains 100 synthetic customer records with two numeric features: Age (years, range 18–65) and Monthly Income (USD, range 850–6 000). Records were designed to form three natural groups representing young low-income customers, middle-aged mid-income customers, and senior high-income customers.

Interpretation

The SSE decreases with each iteration as centroids move closer to the true center of each cluster. When the SSE reduction becomes negligible, the algorithm has converged. After 3 iterations the three customer segments are clearly defined.

FINAL RESULTS

Final Clustering Outcome

Cluster 1
Young / Budget Customers

Ages ~18–30, monthly income ~$850–$1 600. Entry-level earners with limited spending capacity.

Cluster 2
Middle-Aged / Mid-Income

Ages ~30–42, monthly income ~$2 000–$3 300. Established professionals with moderate purchasing power.

Cluster 3
Senior / High Income

Ages ~44–60, monthly income ~$4 400–$6 000. Senior professionals with high disposable income.

Key Takeaways
  • K-Means successfully identified 3 distinct customer segments from unlabeled data.
  • The SSE decreased across all 3 iterations, confirming algorithmic convergence.
  • The clusters align with real-world intuition: age and income are natural segmentation axes.
  • The manual process demonstrates how centroids gradually stabilize as assignments improve.
